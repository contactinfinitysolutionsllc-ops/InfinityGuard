Short version: InfinityGuard collects no personal data whatsoever. Everything runs locally on your device. No account required. No telemetry. No analytics. No data ever leaves your machine except the threat-feed fetches described below — which carry no identifying information.

1. Who We Are

InfinityGuard is a cybersecurity product developed and distributed by Infinity Solutions LLC. This Privacy Policy applies to:

  • The InfinityGuard browser extension (available for Chrome and Chromium-based browsers)
  • The InfinityGuard for Windows desktop application
  • This website at infinityguard.infinityhub.dev

2. Data We Collect

We collect no personal data. InfinityGuard is architected around the principle that your device, your data, and your browsing habits are yours alone. Specifically:

  • We do not collect your name, email, IP address, or any identifying information
  • We do not track which websites you visit
  • We do not log what threats are detected on your machine
  • We do not use analytics, crash-reporting SDKs, or any third-party tracking on this website or inside the product
  • We do not have access to any files scanned by InfinityGuard for Windows
  • We do not create or maintain any user account linked to you

3. What Stays on Your Device

All of the following are stored locally and never transmitted to us:

  • Browser extension settings (toggle states, whitelisted sites, AI provider configuration) — stored in chrome.storage.local on your device only
  • Activity log — the in-popup threat log is session-only, stored in memory, and cleared when you close the browser
  • AI API keys — if you configure the Terms/ToS AI analysis feature, your API key is stored exclusively in chrome.storage.local and is never transmitted to Infinity Solutions LLC
  • Cached threat scores — domain risk scores cached by the extension are session-memory only
  • InfinityGuard for Windows data — scan results, threat logs, and all detection data are stored locally in the application data directory on your machine

4. Network Requests Made by the Extension

The browser extension makes a small number of outbound network requests. None of them include any personal data or identifiers:

Threat intelligence feeds (every 6 hours)

  • URLhaus (urlhaus.abuse.ch) — downloads a public malware domain blocklist. This is a plain HTTPS GET with no cookies, no user identifier, and no request body. Infinity Solutions LLC does not operate this service.
  • OpenPhish (openphish.com) — downloads a public phishing URL list. Same conditions as above.

These fetches are equivalent to downloading a public file. Your IP address is visible to those services' servers as with any internet request, but no InfinityGuard-specific data is attached.

Local IG service (InfinityGuard for Windows users only)

When InfinityGuard for Windows is running, the extension communicates with it over localhost only (http://127.0.0.1:<port>). This traffic never leaves your machine.

AI analysis (optional, user-configured)

If you enable the AI plain-English explanation feature for Terms of Service pages, the extension sends the text of those pages to the AI provider you configure (e.g., Groq, Google Gemini, or a local model via Ollama/LM Studio). Infinity Solutions LLC never sees this data — your request goes directly from your browser to your chosen provider under their privacy policy. We strongly recommend local models for maximum privacy.
